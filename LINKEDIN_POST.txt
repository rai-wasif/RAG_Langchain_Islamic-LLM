🕌 Excited to share my latest AI project: IlmGPT — Islamic Scholar Assistant!

As a CS student passionate about both technology and Islamic knowledge, I built a RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) system that answers questions about Quran and Hadith with authentic source citations — in both English and Urdu.

🔍 The Problem I Solved:
Regular AI chatbots hallucinate when asked about Islam. They make up hadith references, misquote Quran, or give culturally inaccurate answers. IlmGPT solves this by ONLY answering from verified Islamic texts.

⚙️ How It Works:
→ Loaded 6,236 Quran Ayahs + 4,000+ Hadiths (Bukhari, Muslim, Abu Dawud)
→ Converted every text into semantic vectors using SentenceTransformers
→ Stored in ChromaDB vector database for lightning-fast retrieval
→ When you ask a question, the system finds the most relevant Ayahs/Hadiths
→ Sends them to Google Gemini with strict instructions: answer ONLY from these sources
→ Returns bilingual answer (English + Urdu) with full citations like [Quran 2:286] or [Bukhari Hadith 52]

🛠️ Tech Stack:
• LangChain (RAG Framework)
• ChromaDB (Vector Database)
• Google Gemini 1.5 Flash (Free LLM)
• SentenceTransformers (Embeddings)
• Streamlit (Web App)
• Python, Pandas

💡 Key Learning:
Building this taught me that RAG is not just a technical pipeline — it's about trust. In medical, legal, or religious domains, an AI that admits "I don't know" and cites its sources is infinitely more valuable than one that confidently gives wrong answers.
